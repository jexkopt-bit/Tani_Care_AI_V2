## Technical Mandate: The "Build With AI" Requirement
To qualify for evaluation, every project must integrate the Google AI Ecosystem Stack as its core foundation. Solutions will be evaluated based on their transition from "Chat" (simple conversation) to "Action" (Autonomous Execution).
The Intelligence (Brain): Gemini (Flash for speed/low-latency or Pro for complex reasoning).
The Orchestrator: Vertex AI Agent Builder and Firebase Genkit for building Agentic AI workflows.
The Development Lifecycle: Google Cloud Workstations for coding and Google Cloud Run for serverless deployment.
The Context: Vertex AI Search for grounded Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) using national or industrial datasets.

## Mandatory Tools and Deployment:
To qualify every project is required to use Google AI Studio / Google Antigravity and must be deployed on Google Cloud Run.

**Section 1** — Eligibility (Updated)

Open to all Malaysians whether you are a student, a professional developer, or a visionary builder.


1. The Student Category
Eligibility: Ages 16+ (Foundation, Undergraduate, Master’s, and PhD).


2. The Open Category
Eligibility: Anyone aged 16+ and beyond the university level, professionals, industry veterans, hobbyists.


Each participant may only join one team. Registering with or contributing to multiple teams will result in disqualification of all teams involved. 
**Section 2** — Team Requirements 
Team size: Solo to 4 members. Solo submissions are accepted. Teams with more than 4 members at the time of submission will be disqualified. 
Designate one Team Lead. The Team Lead is the primary point of contact for all official communications, submission confirmations, and prize disbursements. 
Team composition is fixed after registration closes. Members may not be added, removed, or substituted after the registration deadline on 20 April 2026. Any changes must be requested in writing before that date. 
Mentors and advisors are not team members. Faculty supervisors, industry mentors, or external coaches are welcome to guide teams but may not contribute directly to code, design, or submission materials.






**Section 3** — Submission Guidelines 
Submission deadline is 21 April 2026 at 11:59 PM. This is a hard stop. No extensions will be granted under any circumstances. Late submissions will be automatically rejected by the system. 
→ Platform: Google Forms
→ Submission Link: To Be Announced
→ Submission Period: 3rd April 2026 - 20 April 2026
All deliverables must be submitted via the official submission portal. Email submissions or links shared outside the portal will not be accepted. 
Required submission package:

**Section 4** — Code of Conduct & Plagiarism Policy 
Zero tolerance for plagiarism. Copying code, designs, or content from other teams — or from previous hackathon projects — without attribution is grounds for immediate disqualification. All code will be screened for plagiarism. 
AI-generated code must be disclosed. Teams are permitted to use AI coding tools (GitHub Copilot, Gemini, etc.) but must declare this in the README. The team must be able to explain and defend every part of their codebase during judging. 
Respectful conduct is mandatory. Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind — based on gender, race, religion, nationality, disability, or any other characteristic — will not be tolerated and will result in immediate removal from the event. 
No harassment policy. Any participant who experiences or witnesses harassment must report it immediately to the organising committee. All reports will be treated confidentially. 
Participants must attend mandatory sessions. Finalists (Top 50) are required to attend both days of the Final Event (16–17 May 2026) in person. Failure to attend without prior written notice may result in disqualification. 
Responsible use of AI. All projects must comply with ethical AI principles. Projects that exhibit bias, privacy violations, or potential for harm will be penalised during judging.

**Section 6** — Disqualification Conditions
